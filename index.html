Intreresting facts about Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Intreresting facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.
  • Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long.
  • Chameleons are tree-huggers.

Intreresting facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Intreresting facts about Teddy

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Intreresting facts about Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • They spend a lot of their day eating.
  • Cubs are well protected in their first month.

Intreresting facts about Cicada

  • Cicadas Live on All Continents Except Antarctica.
  • They Have One of the Longest Insect Life Spans.
  • Most of Their Lives Is Spent Underground.
  • They Only Emerge When the Ground Is 64 Degrees.
